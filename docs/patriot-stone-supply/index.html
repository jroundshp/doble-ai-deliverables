05 Priority Recommendations
01 Claim & Build a Google Business Profile (Highest Priority)
This is the single biggest lever and it's free. Create and verify a Google Business Profile with the real yard address, hours, service area, product categories, and photos of the yard and trucks. It's what puts Patriot Stone on Maps, in the local "3-pack," and in "gravel near me" results, and it's a primary source AI assistants cite for local recommendations. Nothing else in this list matters as much.
02 Publish the Address & a Consistent NAP Everywhere
Put the yard's Name, Address, and Phone on the website (header, footer, and a real Contact page with an embedded map) and make it match exactly across Google, Apple Maps, Bing, and Yelp. Decide whether to move to a local Denton (940/469) number or clearly present the current one as the business line — the out-of-state area code muddies the local signal. Consistent NAP is the bedrock of local ranking.
03 Fix the SEO Foundation (Titles, Descriptions, H1s)
Replace the duplicated title with a real, location-keyword title on every page ("Crushed Stone, Gravel & Aggregate Delivery in Denton, TX | Patriot Stone Supply"), add unique meta descriptions and Open Graph tags, and give each page a specific H1. This is a couple of hours of work with an SEO plugin and it's the difference between being readable by search and being skipped.
04 Add LocalBusiness & Product Schema
Add LocalBusiness (or Store) JSON-LD with the address, hours, geo-coordinates, and service area, plus Product schema for the material types. This is how Google and AI tools read the business as structured data — and it directly helps disambiguate Patriot Stone from the unrelated "Patriot Stone" companies in other states that currently muddy its search results.
05 Turn On a Review Engine
Start from zero deliberately: text a Google review link to every satisfied customer, add a QR code to invoices and the counter, and enable recommendations on Facebook. Even 10–15 genuine reviews would leapfrog the current "no social proof at all" position and feed both Maps ranking and AI citations. For a supplier that lives on reliability, reviews are the trust currency.
06 Publish Materials, Delivery-Area & "We Buy Concrete" Content + FAQ (GEO)
Give each material its own indexable page (coverage, uses, pricing guidance) and build out the concrete-recycling service as its own real page — it's the least-contested, most ownable message in the market. Add an FAQ answering the questions buyers actually type: delivery zips, minimum loads, coverage per yard, what's accepted at the recycling. This is the content that wins both Google and AI answers.
07 Fix Mobile Zoom & Add Alt Text
Remove maximum-scale=1.0, user-scalable=0 from the viewport so contractors on phones can pinch-to-zoom — an accessibility requirement and a usability win for a mobile-first, on-the-jobsite audience. Add descriptive alt text to the product and yard photos. Fast, cheap, and good for both users and search. (Worth fixing across all of the owner's sites, since they share this flaw.)
08 Point Instagram at the Funnel + Add an AI Quote Concierge
Put the website and a "call/text for a quote" link in the Instagram bio, tag the Denton location on every reel, and cross-post to Facebook so the one active channel actually drives leads. Then add an AI chat concierge to the site that answers material, coverage, delivery-area, and "we buy concrete" questions and captures quote requests 24/7 — the exact after-hours questions a phone-only yard misses.
