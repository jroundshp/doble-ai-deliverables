Headline Finding — Two Websites Competing With Each Other

Walker Welding is running two separate live websites for one business — walkerweldingdenco.com and walkerwelds.com — with the same phone number and the same email. Google can't tell which one is the "real" site, so any authority the business earns is split between them, and neither ranks. On top of that, the newer, better-looking site is branded around Denver while the business is actually based in Eagle — pointing customers and search engines at the wrong market. The single biggest win here is to consolidate into one site, anchored to your real Eagle County home base.

06 Priority Recommendations
01Consolidate to One Website
Pick one site and commit to it — the recommended path is to keep the cleaner "DenCo" design and copy but rebuild it around your real Eagle County identity, then redirect the other domain to it. One strong, focused site stops the two from cannibalizing each other and lets all your search authority compound in one place.
02Own "Eagle," Not "Denver"
Re-anchor the brand and site to Eagle County — the market you're actually in and can realistically win. Update the naming, headlines, and service-area language to Eagle, Vail, Gypsum, Edwards, and the surrounding valley. It matches your address, your phone's 970 area code, and even the "remote logging site" review — and puts you in a far less crowded market than Denver metro.
03Fully Work the Google Business Profile You Already Have
You're not starting from zero — the profile exists and already carries a flawless 5.0 across 9 reviews. Now make it work harder: confirm you have management access, complete the categories, services, and 24-hour emergency hours, load a strong set of real job photos, point it at your one consolidated site, and post regularly. A fully built-out profile is the #1 driver of "welder near me," Maps results, and the local answers AI assistants cite — and yours has a great head start.
04Add Schema, Meta Descriptions & Rankable Pages
On the consolidated site, add LocalBusiness structured data (name, phone, area served, hours, reviews), keyword-rich meta descriptions, and Open Graph tags so shared links show a proper preview. Then break the single page into focused, rankable pages — "Custom Steel Fabrication," "Trailer & Equipment Repair," "24-Hour Mobile Welding in Eagle County" — so the site can show up for the specific jobs people search.
05Launch a Review Engine
Move from three static reviews to a live, growing Google profile. Text every satisfied customer a one-tap review link right after the job. Review volume is both a trust signal and a ranking and AI-citation factor — the practical way to pass established competitors in the wider market and dominate the local one.
