06 Market Opportunity — Eagle River Valley & Roaring Fork
Drought Context — 2026

The Eagle River Valley and Roaring Fork Valley are currently experiencing severe to extreme drought conditions, amplifying wildfire risk across the region. Prolonged dry years increase standing dead timber — beetle-kill, drought-stress mortality — raise fuel loads in the understory, and trigger insurance pressure on homeowners and HOAs to establish defensible space. Many property owners face HB 1182 insurance compliance deadlines, creating an urgency-driven sales environment that benefits operators who are discoverable online.

Fire Mitigation — High Urgency Now

Demand runs on two tracks simultaneously:

  • Large-scale commercial/government — ranches, HOAs, municipalities, and federal land managers contracting multi-acre fuel reduction. High value, competitive bid, relationship-driven.
  • Residential defensible space — individual homeowners in Eagle, Garfield, Pitkin, and Routt counties receiving insurance notices. High volume, faster sales cycle, strong word-of-mouth potential in tight mountain communities.
Timber Clearing — Durable Base

Large timber clearing — beetle-kill removal, construction site clearing, ranch management — remains a durable part of the high country market. These jobs don't go away.

This segment is more relationship-driven than urgency-driven. Existing operator relationships likely hold this business even without digital fixes — but fire mitigation is where new revenue is actively being won and lost right now.
