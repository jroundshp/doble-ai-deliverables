05 Priority Recommendations
01 Maximize the Google Profile You Already Have
Alpine's Google Business Profile is its strongest asset — 4.6★ across 65 reviews, with hours, photos, and order-online already live. Now compound it: post weekly (specials, events, seasonal menus), add the full menu and product tiles, respond to every review, and keep fresh photos flowing. An actively managed profile ranks higher in Maps and "near me" results and is a primary source AI assistants cite for local recommendations.
02 Install an SEO Foundation (Titles, Descriptions, Schema)
Add an SEO plugin and give every page a real title, meta description, and Open Graph tags, plus a specific H1. Add Restaurant / CafeOrCoffeeShop and Menu JSON-LD schema so Google and AI tools can read the name, address, hours, and menu as structured data. The HTML menu is already a strength — schema makes it a citable one.
03 Fix Mobile Zoom & Add Alt Text
Remove maximum-scale=1.0, user-scalable=0 from the viewport so phone visitors can pinch-to-zoom — an accessibility requirement and a usability win for the mobile-first café crowd. Add descriptive alt text to menu and space photos. Fast, cheap, and good for both users and search.
04 Keep the Review Engine Turning
Build on the 65 Google reviews already there with table tents and a receipt QR asking happy guests for a review — a steady flow directly feeds Maps ranking and AI citations, and after 18 months with real regulars those reviews are there to be asked for. Broadening onto Yelp or a TripAdvisor listing would add cross-platform signals, but there's essentially no TripAdvisor presence today, so whether to tackle it is the owners' call.
05 Publish an FAQ + Coffee/Breakfast Content (GEO)
Add an FAQ (hours, wifi and seating, vegan/gluten-free options, whether the wine bar runs weeknights) and a light blog around the family bakery, seasonal menus, and the space. This is the indexable, question-answering content that wins "best coffee/breakfast in Ellicottville" in both Google and AI answers.
06 Connect the Bake Shop, Bistro & Airbnb
Cross-link Alpine Bistro, the Ellicottville Bake Shop, and the family Airbnb across every site and social account, and put a welcome guide in the Airbnb that sends guests to the café and bakery. It compounds several audiences, strengthens each business's local SEO, and turns a captive stream of overnight visitors into paying customers — all for the cost of a few links and a coordinated post.
07 Turn Instagram Into a Traffic & Review Driver
The inherited ~1,739-follower audience is the strongest asset — now direct it. Put the ordering link and a "leave us a review" prompt in the bio and stories, keep coffee/pastry reels going, and cross-tag the bakery and Ellicottville tourism accounts. Convert engaged followers into reviews, orders, and search signals, not just likes.
08 Add an AI Concierge for Visitors & Guests
Add an AI chat concierge to the site that answers hours, menu, allergen, seating, and ordering questions 24/7 — the exact things a tourist or an Airbnb guest asks before they walk in. It captures after-hours interest a small team can't field live and funnels it into a visit. This is Doble AI's signature layer.
