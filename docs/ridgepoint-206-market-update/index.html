02 · The Market Right Now

Real interest, not yet real urgency

The honest read on Beaver Creek this season is that buyers are watching closely but moving slowly. That is true at Ridgepoint and across every price point in Eagle County right now.

The resort came to a near standstill the week Beaver Creek and Vail closed, which is normal for spring. Ridgepoint is almost entirely second homes, so its buyers are not local families, they are owners who come back to the valley as summer arrives. Online views and saved listings tell us they are looking. What we have not yet seen is the volume of showings, open-house visits or offers we normally get at Ridgepoint by mid-summer.

On the Zillow activity you asked about: a save means someone has added your home to a personal shortlist, a genuine signal of interest, though the viewer stays anonymous to us. Across all five current Ridgepoint listings, view counts track closely with days on market, and the lower-priced units draw the most views and saves simply because more buyers can reach that price. Overall the numbers are modest, and interest online has not yet converted into appointments.

How to read Zillow "saves"

A save = a buyer building a shortlist, often before they call a broker or plan a trip. It confirms interest. It is not yet an appointment, and we cannot see who they are. Consistent saves at your price tier are a good sign heading into the season.

Over the winter, snow was the only elixir that would loosen buyer hesitation. Right now it is rain, and lots of it. John Tyler · Slifer Smith & Frampton

A dry, fire-conscious spring followed by a wet early summer has kept urgency low. We fully expect this to shift over the coming month as summer events begin, brokers return from their May travels, and second-home buyers turn their attention back to Eagle County. The interest is real. The season simply has not caught up to it yet.

05 · The Complete Picture

Where the whole valley stands mid-year

Zoom out from Ridgepoint and the same story holds valley-wide: more inventory, patient buyers with leverage, and resort-core properties holding their value while entry-level and down-valley segments feel the pressure.

The county median eased about 17% year over year, but that figure is driven by mix, fewer very-high-end closings early in the year, not by a broad cut to what homes are worth. In the luxury tier that matters most to Ridgepoint, the $5M-plus segment actually deepened, and its days on market improved sharply, from roughly 249 to 152. Prices inside the gates of Beaver Creek are far more about scarcity and ski access than about the county-wide trend line.

For a seller, the takeaway is simple. Buyers have options and patience, so pricing to the market and showing the home at its best is what moves it. Homes that do both still sell readily, especially in the resort core. That is exactly the position we have #206 set up for.
