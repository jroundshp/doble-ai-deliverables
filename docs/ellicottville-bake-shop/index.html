Critical Issue — Requires Immediate Attention

The website's sitemap.xml — the file Google uses to crawl the site — is polluted with roughly 150 junk "product listing" URLs that have nothing to do with a bakery: winter snow boots, an engine blower motor, a "breast milk refrigerator," a tactical laser, egress ladders. These pages now return 404 Not Found, so Google is being handed 150 dead links to crawl instead of the real menu, about, and events pages. Something injected these — most likely a rogue or abandoned plugin, or a site compromise — and confirming the exact cause would take a look inside WordPress. Either way, it wastes crawl budget, drags down the whole domain's quality signal in Google's eyes, and should be cleaned out and the site security-hardened right away.

01 Business Snapshot
What It Is
A full artisan bakery and breakfast/lunch café. Rotating daily scratch breads (sourdough, rye, brioche, honey wheat, baguette), pastries (croissants, scones, cinnamon rolls, pain au chocolat, sticky buns), and signature breakfast sandwiches on house-made biscuits and breads — plus coffee. Cozy 30+ seat room with a fireplace.
Location & Contact
5 E Washington St., Suite 5a, Ellicottville, NY 14731 · (716) 699-9116 · hello@ellicottvillebakeshop.com. Hours appear to be roughly Wed–Mon mornings into early afternoon — but listings disagree across sources, which is itself a fixable findability problem (see recommendations).
The Story
Owners Corey and Jennifer Hogan were inspired by a bakery in Iceland, then toured bakeries across Europe and the U.S. before opening in March 2024. Everything is baked fresh from scratch daily. A clear, quotable origin story — a real asset for both search and AI answers.
Local Sourcing
Ingredients sourced from Valley View Farms, Eden Valley Creamery, and Troll Bridge Farms. Community-driven from day one — the owners polled residents on opening hours to serve the pre-work and pre-ski crowd. This "local, from scratch" narrative is exactly what ranks and gets cited when it's written down and structured.
Ordering & Catering
Online ordering runs through Toast (indexable and transactional — a genuine strength). Catering available for larger orders. The machine to take an order already works; the gap is upstream, in how many people find their way to it.
Owner Portfolio
The Hogans also own Alpine Bistro up Washington Street and run a local Airbnb short-term rental. It's a small family of businesses, not a set of rivals — yet none of them link to or cross-promote each other online. Airbnb guests and each shop's customers are audiences that could be feeding one another, kept separate.

04 Online Presence Audit
Website (ellicottvillebakeshop.com)C
The real pages are good — a deep menu, a well-told about page, events and news sections. The content bones are there. But the site runs on WordPress with no SEO plugin, a spam-polluted sitemap, and no structured data, so all that good content is working with one hand tied behind its back.
Technical SEOF
No meta description, no Open Graph tags, no Twitter cards, no JSON-LD schema, and zero H1 tags on the homepage — plus ~150 spam 404 URLs in the sitemap. The fundamentals search engines and AI crawlers rely on to understand a page are almost entirely absent. High-impact, low-cost to fix.

For a café the GBP is the #1 driver of "bakery / breakfast near me" and a primary AI source — and this one is genuinely healthy. Key Finding

Ellicottville Bake Shop has already won the part money can't easily buy — people love the place, and it shows in a 4.4★ Google profile with over 100 reviews. What it hasn't done is finish the job on its own website: TripAdvisor sits empty and Yelp is light, there's no FAQ or blog for AI tools to quote, and the site is quietly being sabotaged by a spam-injected sitemap. Every one of those is low-cost and fast to fix, and because the product and the ordering system already work, better visibility flows almost directly to more orders through the door.

05 Priority Recommendations
01 Clean the Spam & Secure the Site — Now
Remove the ~150 junk product URLs and regenerate a clean sitemap that lists only the real pages. Something injected these — we'd recommend investigating what caused it (a rogue or abandoned plugin, or a compromise) with a look inside WordPress, then hardening security (updates, strong admin credentials, a security plugin). This stops the domain-quality bleed and is the first thing Google needs to see fixed.
02 Install an SEO Foundation (Titles, Descriptions, Schema)
Add an SEO plugin and give every page a real title, a meta description, Open Graph tags, and a proper H1. Then add LocalBusiness / Bakery and Menu JSON-LD schema so Google and AI tools can read the name, address, hours, and menu as structured data. This is the cheapest, highest-leverage ranking work available.
03 Keep the Google Profile Active & Optimized
The profile is already claimed and strong (4.4★, 105 reviews) — now maximize it. Lock in one consistent set of hours (the conflicting listings need reconciling), confirm the Toast ordering link, post weekly (specials, seasonal breads, events), respond to reviews, and keep fresh photos of the bread, pastries, and room flowing. An actively managed profile ranks higher in "near me" and Maps results and is a primary feed for AI local answers.
04 Launch a Review Engine
Google is already strong at 105 reviews — the gap is everywhere else. Claim the empty TripAdvisor listing and the thin Yelp page, and keep the Google flywheel turning with a QR code at the register and on the bag plus a Toast follow-up. Spreading reviews across platforms is what deepens trust and gives AI assistants the cross-verified signals they weigh — Google alone isn't enough as these answers mature.
05 Add an FAQ + Blog Layer (GEO)
Publish an FAQ answering what people actually ask — gluten-free options, when fresh bread comes out, custom and catering orders, parking, ski-morning hours — plus a simple blog (new seasonal breads, the local farms you source from, the Iceland story). This is exactly the indexable, question-answering content that earns organic traffic and gets quoted in ChatGPT and Google AI answers.
06 Connect the Bake Shop, Bistro & Airbnb
Cross-link the Bake Shop, Alpine Bistro, and the family Airbnb across every website and social account ("our sister spots up the street"), and put a welcome guide in the Airbnb that sends guests straight to the bakery and café for breakfast. It compounds several audiences into one, strengthens local SEO across all of them, and turns a captive stream of overnight visitors into paying customers — for the cost of a few links and a coordinated post.
07 Turn Instagram Into a Traffic & Review Driver
The strongest channel is already working — now point it somewhere. Add the Toast ordering link and a "leave us a review" prompt to the bio and stories, keep the fresh-bread Reels coming, and tag Alpine Bistro and Ellicottville tourism accounts. Convert ~1,725 engaged followers into orders and Google reviews, not just likes.
08 Add an After-Hours AI Concierge
An AI chat concierge on the site — answering hours, allergen, custom-order, and catering questions and pointing customers to Toast ordering 24/7 — captures the tourist and after-hours interest a small team can't field live during a morning rush. It can answer the same questions for Airbnb guests planning their stay. This is Doble AI's signature layer.
