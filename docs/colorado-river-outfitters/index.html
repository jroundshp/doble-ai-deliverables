Log into TripAdvisor, claim the business profile, and correct the status. This is the single most damaging item currently active against you — every prospect who finds CRO on TripAdvisor sees a closed business and books a competitor. Takes approximately 15 minutes to fix.
02 Claim and Build Your Google Business Profile
Set up or claim the GBP with one consistent address (Edwards, CO). Complete every field — photos, services, hours, booking link. A fully-built GBP is the #1 driver of Google Maps visibility and AI search citations. It's free, and the impact compounds over time as reviews accumulate.
03 Put the 2025 Best of Vail Valley Win to Work
The BOVV badge belongs on your website hero, your Instagram bio, your Facebook About section, your email signature, and every booking confirmation email — for the next 12 months at minimum. Trout Trickers built their brand on this award over five years. You just won it. Use it.
04 Fix the Blog 404 — Then Publish Eagle River Fishing Reports
The blog link in your main navigation returns a 404 error — fix this first. Then start publishing regular fishing reports. Vail Valley Anglers and Fly Fishing Outfitters dominate organic and AI search largely because they publish these reports consistently. Your guides know the rivers better than anyone. That knowledge is content, and content is search presence.
05 Build a Post-Trip Review Request System
After every trip, send a brief follow-up email to clients: "Hope you had a great day on the water — would you take two minutes to leave us a Google review?" Link directly to your GBP review page. Five intentional requests per guide per month would triple your review count within 90 days and directly improve your Google Maps ranking and AI search presence.
06 Unify Your Address Across All Platforms
TripAdvisor shows Bond, CO. Yelp shows Edwards, CO. Your website references Vail Valley and Glenwood Springs. Pick one format (Edwards, CO is most accurate) and update it everywhere — website footer, Google, Yelp, TripAdvisor, Facebook. Google suppresses businesses with inconsistent NAP data across platforms.
07 Activate Instagram Consistently
Fly fishing is one of the most visual categories on social media. Big trout, float trips through Colorado canyons, guide personalities, morning light on the Eagle River — this content is already happening on every trip. It just isn't being captured and posted. Three posts per week would put CRO in front of the exact audience that books guided trips, and the visual quality of your product sells itself.
