The opportunity

Adjust to the Demand Pricing Calendar.

At a flat $350, the apartment is too expensive on quiet April midweeks (so it sits empty) and too cheap on the weekends that sell out no matter what, like Fall Festival, Mardi Gras, and Christmas week. Both are lost revenue.

The fix has two parts. First, a demand calendar that prices every night to what the village is actually worth that night. Second, the lever only you have: when a night is soft, you can send up a breakfast sandwich and a custom coffee from your own café instead of dropping the rate, protecting your price while still winning the booking.

What follows is the full model, then a simple way for us to build and run it with you.

Flat $350 vs. the tiered model

A single flat rate is two mistakes at once: too high midweek and in mud season, so those nights sit empty, and too low on the weekends that sell out anyway. Same apartment, more total revenue, just by moving the number to match the night.

04

The Café, Bistro & Bake Shop advantage

The lever no other Ellicottville listing has

You own the café next door. When a night is soft, the reflex is to drop the rate. Don't. Drop in the breakfast sandwich, the coffee, the beer, the wine instead. A welcome from your own businesses reads as a generous perk, holds your nightly rate intact, and costs you food cost, not room revenue.

You're not limited to breakfast, either. Across the Bistro, Ellicottville Coffee, and the Bake Shop, you already stock everything a memorable welcome needs:

Breakfast sandwiches

The everyday closer for a soft midweek or a family booking.

Custom coffees

Your own Ellicottville Coffee, made to order. The detail guests remember.

Beer & wine

A welcome bottle on a ski weekend is what turns a stay into a five-star review.

It protects your rate

Discounts erode your going rate and are hard to raise back. A bundled perk keeps the listing at full price and still lifts bookings.

The math favors you

A welcome that reads like $25–$50 off costs you roughly $4 a guest. See the table.

Only you can offer it

Guests stay in the heart of the village with your café one door down. "Wake up to a breakfast sandwich and a custom coffee, on us" is a headline no competitor can copy.

It feeds your other businesses

Guests step next door for the free sandwich, stay for lunch, walk up to the Bake Shop, come back for dinner. The perk doubles as a funnel into everything you run.

The play: don't stock the perk in the room. Invite them in.

A bottle left on the counter is a nice touch, and then it's over. A card they redeem in person is a reason to walk into Alpine Bistro on day one.

Every guest becomes a customer who meets your team, sees the menu, and usually spends past the free item. Same perk, except now it drives foot traffic instead of sitting in the room.

Then extend the deal to the whole building.

Trattoria Partenope sits directly below the rental, and they're your tenant. Bring them into the package. A second card sends your guests down for a glass of wine or a dessert on the house, filling Trattoria's tables with people who are already ten steps away.

In return, Trattoria posts your Airbnb and Ellicottville Outside to their followers a few times a season. Now the building markets itself: you feed their restaurant, they feed your bookings, and every guest touches all of it before they check out.
