The Core Finding: A Content Engine With the Wiring Left Unfinished

iConnect Training publishes a real blog post every month, hand-writes its page titles, and already owns roughly six of the top nine Google results for "refrigeration training unit." Yet the company has no visible Google Business Profile, none of its 50 product pages carry Product schema, and every page's structured data still points at a leftover GoDaddy staging address (256.f93.myftpupload.com) instead of the live site. Google is being told, on every single page, that this website belongs to a construction site that no longer exists. These are finishing details, and they are exactly the details that decide who gets surfaced in Maps, rich results, and AI answers.

03 · Where They Stand

Strengths and Gaps

Working in Their Favor

  • A real content engine. 84 blog posts, one per month like clockwork, written for their actual buyer (the instructor). Newest post July 13, 2026.
  • Long-tail dominance. Roughly 6 of the top 9 Google results for "refrigeration training unit" are iconnecttraining.com pages.
  • Clean on-page SEO. Unique hand-written titles and meta descriptions on 10 of 11 sampled pages. Sitemap, canonicals, and image alt text all in place.
  • Credibility assets. 16 testimonials, an FAQ page, a product matrix, trade-show presence, grant awards, and an active YouTube channel (AHR 2026 recap already posted).
  • They own their branded Google results. Page one for "iConnect Training" is all theirs.

Working Against Them

  • Not visible on Google Maps. No Business Profile panel appears for either company name, on Google or Bing. A 29-person manufacturer with a real HQ and zero Google reviews.
  • No Product schema. 50 product pages, none marked up as products. No Organization schema either. Invisible to rich results and AI shopping-style answers.
  • Staging-domain bug. Site-wide structured data references a defunct GoDaddy staging URL.
  • Zoom disabled on mobile. The viewport blocks pinch-to-zoom, a WCAG accessibility failure, on a site selling to educators and government buyers.
  • Crowded name on Bing. "iConnect" collides with HR portals and government systems; they rank about 5th for their own name plus town.
  • Stale edges. Parent site npinnovations.com untouched for about 17 months, team pages frozen since February 2023, and typos live in meta descriptions ("neeeds," "principals").

Twelve Channels, Graded

Website Design & UX

Professional, current, and organized around the buyer: product matrix, equipment packages, curriculum, videos. Heavy builder stack (The7 plus WPBakery) but it works, and server response is fast.

B+

On-Page SEO

Unique, descriptive, clearly hand-written titles and metas across the site. Docked for two spelling errors in live meta descriptions, a generic "Portfolio Archive" page with no meta, and an og:title of "Home" on the homepage.

A-

Content Engine

84 posts at a steady monthly cadence with zero gaps, aimed squarely at HVAC instructors ("How to Spot Gaps in Your HVAC/R Lab Before the School Year Starts," July 13). This is the asset everything else should be built on.

B

Organic Search Rankings

Dominant on product-specific terms, present but outranked by dealers and aggregators on broad buying terms. The gap is content positioned for the purchasing decision-maker, not just the instructor.

F*

Google Business Profile & Maps

No local panel visible for either company name on Google or Bing, and zero Google reviews. *We report "not visible," not "does not exist": an unclaimed or suspended profile may sit behind this, which only the owner can confirm. Either way, the public-facing result is invisibility.

D

Structured Data

SEO-plugin defaults only. No Product or Offer markup on any of 50 product pages, no Organization schema, no FAQPage schema, and a site-wide reference to a dead GoDaddy staging domain inside the schema graph.

C-

AI Search Readiness (GEO)

The raw material is there: an FAQ page with 10 real questions, comparison posts (TU-9240 vs TU-9250), testimonials. None of it is marked up or structured so AI assistants can cite it. When a district administrator asks ChatGPT for HVAC lab equipment vendors, this content should be the answer.

C+

Social Media

All four channels exist and show 2026 activity (YouTube AHR recap, Facebook posts, Instagram, LinkedIn showcase page). But 219 LinkedIn followers is thin for a company selling to institutions, and the effort is not compounding.

B-

Reviews & Social Proof

16 on-site testimonials and a "Real Stories From Real Instructors" series are genuinely good. Third-party proof is near zero: no Google reviews (no profile), and nothing visible anywhere a skeptical procurement officer would check independently.

C

Mobile & Accessibility

Pinch-to-zoom is disabled (a WCAG 1.4.4 failure). The homepage ships 326 KB of HTML with 26 stylesheets and 17 external scripts. Educational and government buyers increasingly carry accessibility procurement requirements; this is a sales risk, not just a technical one.

C+

Brand Architecture

Four domains (iconnecttraining, marcraft, npinnovations, imanifold) with duplicated press releases, weak cross-linking, and a parent site 17 months stale. On Bing, the crowded "iConnect" name buries them below HR-software collisions.

B-

Conversion Path

Quote-request forms on product pages and a print catalog offer fit the industry. But there is no email link anywhere, no office hours, and no dealer or rep locator, so every path funnels into one form or one phone line.

05 · Priority Recommendations

Eight Fixes, In Order

01Stand Up the Google Business ProfileFreeWeek 1
Claim or create the profile as a manufacturer at 6442 Route 242 East, then ask five long-standing instructor customers for reviews. This is the single largest visibility gap, and it costs nothing.
02Purge the Staging Domain From the Schema GraphWeek 1
Run a search-and-replace for 256.f93.myftpupload.com across the database and re-verify the SEO plugin's site settings. Every page currently misidentifies itself to Google.
03Add Product Schema to All 50 Training-Unit Pages
Name, image, brand, model, and description on every unit, templated once through the portfolio post type. This is how product pages qualify for rich results, and how AI assistants learn the catalog exists.
04Mark Up the FAQ and Comparison Content for AI Search
FAQPage schema on the existing FAQ page, FAQ blocks on each category page, and Organization schema tying together all four domains and social profiles. The content already answers buyer questions; the markup lets machines cite it.
05Remove the Zoom Lock and Run a Performance Pass
Delete maximum-scale and user-scalable from the viewport tag (a one-line fix for the WCAG failure), then consolidate the 26 stylesheets and 17 scripts the builder stack ships on every page.
06Build Decision-Maker Landing Pages to Win Head Terms
"HVAC training equipment for schools" and "HVAC lab for community colleges" pages written for administrators and procurement, with funding guidance (Perkins V, NYSERDA-style grants) that the monthly blog can feed. Dealers are winning these terms today with thinner content.
07Tidy the Brand Edges
Fix the meta typos and the "Home" og:title, give the training-units archive a real title and description, refresh or consolidate npinnovations.com (17 months idle), and update the team pages (frozen since 2023).
08Turn the Grant Story Into Third-Party Press
The $250K NYSERDA award and the woman-owned manufacturer story exist only as self-published releases. Pitch trade press and WNY business media; independent coverage builds the citations and authority the crowded "iConnect" name needs, especially on Bing.

The hard part is already done.

North Park Innovations publishes consistently, ranks for what it makes, and has a genuine story: a family-owned, woman-owned Ellicottville manufacturer shipping training labs worldwide. What's missing is the connective layer that turns that work into visibility where buying decisions now start: Google Maps, rich results, and AI answers. Most of these fixes are one-time, technical, and permanent. A company scoring 6.8 with these gaps open has an unusually short path to 9.
