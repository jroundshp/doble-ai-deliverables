01 Your Snapshot
Who You Are
Cody Richardson, a Colorado native and former firefighter who went full-time as an artist in 2015, working out of Windsor, Colorado. Ann Richardson runs operations. Together you turn authentic vintage license plates into handmade gamefish and wildlife wall art.
What You Make
One-of-a-kind license-plate sculptures matched to where the fish is caught: a Bahamas plate becomes a bonefish, a rusty Rocky Mountain plate a trout, a Florida plate a tarpon, a Cuban plate a permit. Plus hunting species, hats, fly boxes, and accessories.
The Backstory
It started in 2015 with a green-and-white Colorado trout you made for your own wall. Three fly shops took a chance on six pieces; four sold before they were even hung. It grew from there into shops nationwide. A genuinely distinctive story, and one your website barely tells.
How You Sell
Two channels: direct-to-consumer on your own Shopify store, and wholesale through fly shops across the U.S. and internationally, with drop-ship and no minimums. The wholesale network is a real strength; the direct channel is where the margin is.
Reputation
Real earned attention: features in The Drake Magazine and High Country Angler, and a full episode on The Articulate Fly podcast. Inside fly fishing, the work speaks for itself. That credibility just isn't captured anywhere a new buyer would see it.
The Inventory Edge
18,000 to 20,000 authentic plates on hand, most pre-1980s, sourced from pickers, collectors, and international travel. It's a moat no competitor can copy, and a story worth telling far more loudly than it is today.

05 Priority Recommendations
01Publish The Story You Already Live
This is the rare business where the content is genuinely interesting. Every piece has an article in it: why a Bahamas plate makes the perfect bonefish, the provenance of a 1970s Colorado trout, the firefighter-to-artist story, how you source 18,000 plates. Revive the blog and publish steadily. This is what earns organic search traffic for the gift and art terms you're invisible for today, and it's the exact content foundation we build for our clients.
02Own The AI-Search Frontier Before Anyone Else In The Niche
No one making fish art has structured their content for AI assistants, so when someone asks ChatGPT or Gemini for a unique fishing gift, generic shops get named and you don't. A well-organized set of answers about your work, your process, and buying custom art would make you the artist these tools cite. It's the newest and most defensible ground in the whole report, and it goes to whoever claims it first.
03Put Your Proof On The Page
You've earned real credibility that no buyer sees at the moment of decision. Add customer reviews to your product pages, build a simple review-request into every order, and add an "as seen in" strip for The Drake, High Country Angler, and The Articulate Fly. A shoppable map of the fly shops that carry you turns your wholesale network into on-site social proof. The trust already exists; this puts it where it converts.
04Close The Two FAQ Gaps That Cost Sales
Your FAQ is good but silent on shipping and returns, and on a $350 to $1,200 handmade piece those are the exact questions that decide whether someone clicks buy. Adding clear, reassuring answers removes friction at checkout and, as a bonus, gives AI assistants clean, quotable content about how buying from you works.
05Shift The Mix Toward Direct Sales
Wholesale built the brand and should continue, but every wholesale piece hands roughly half the margin to the shop. The three moves above, content, AI visibility, and proof, all funnel toward the same outcome: more of the demand you already generate converting on your own site at full margin. Even a modest shift from wholesale to direct is meaningful money on pieces at this price point.
