01 Community Snapshot
What It Is
A private, gated mountain community spanning 7,000 acres in the heart of Colorado's Vail Valley. 817 homesites across five distinct neighborhoods, with 600+ developed homes. One of the most comprehensive private amenity packages in the Rocky Mountain West.
Location
Edwards, CO 81632 — 25 miles east of Vail along the I-70 corridor. Eagle County, Vail Valley. 140 miles west of Denver International Airport and 30 miles from Eagle County Regional Airport.
Signature Amenities
Three private championship golf courses designed by Jack Nicklaus, Hale Irwin, and Tom Fazio. Athletic Center with indoor saltwater pool. Equestrian Center. 33 miles of maintained hiking & biking trails. Private fly fishing on 1.5 miles of Eagle River plus four stocked ponds. Cordillera Vail Gondola Club ski access.
Neighborhoods
Five distinct areas — Summit, Ranch, Divide, Territories, and Valley Club — ranging from 7,200 ft elevation at Valley Club to 9,000 ft at the Summit. Each neighborhood has its own character and relationship to the land.
Governing Bodies
Cordillera Property Owners Association (CPOA) — GM Crystal Wilson, (970) 688-8850. Cordillera Metro District (CMD) — GM Trevor Broersma, (970) 926-1923. CPOA manages amenities and programming; CMD handles roads, utilities, and public safety.
Property Values
Homes: $800,000–$6,000,000. Vacant lots: from $75,000. The Valley Club is notable as the only true golf cart lifestyle community in the Vail Valley from Vail to Wolcott. Nearly 65% of the 7,000 acres is dedicated open space.

03 Where They Stand

Cordillera is objectively one of the finest mountain communities in the Rockies — three championship golf courses, private Eagle River frontage, world-class fitness and equestrian amenities on 7,000 acres. The product is extraordinary. The digital presence is barely visible. A prospective buyer doing due diligence on a $2M purchase finds real estate broker pages, a closed hotel listing, and a member-gated newsletter — not an authoritative, compelling community voice.

Strengths
Three championship golf courses (Nicklaus, Irwin, Fazio). Private fly fishing on 1.5 miles of Eagle River. Equestrian Center. 33 miles of trails. Indoor saltwater pool. Ski access via Cordillera Vail Gondola Club. Functional community website with governance and service info. Dedicated staff and established HOA infrastructure.

Website is entirely resident-facing — no public-facing marketing narrative. Google Business Profile absent. Golf course TripAdvisor listings unclaimed with only 20 total reviews. Social media has fewer followers than many local restaurants. The highest-visibility Yelp result is a permanently-closed hotel. No public content stream whatsoever.
Biggest Opportunity
An AI-powered resident and prospect chatbot would serve both audiences — answering common questions 24/7 while giving prospective buyers an authoritative, immediate introduction to the community. Combined with public content and a managed review strategy, this could transform Cordillera's discovery path for the buyers who matter most.

05 Priority Recommendations
01 Launch an AI-Powered Resident & Prospect Chatbot
The single highest-ROI digital investment Cordillera could make. An AI chatbot on cordilleraliving.com would answer "What are the annual dues?" "How do I get gate access?" "What golf memberships are available?" for both current residents and prospective buyers — 24/7, without staff time. For prospects making a $2M decision, having immediate, accurate answers is a conversion driver. For residents, it reduces the repetitive inquiry load on CPOA staff.
02 Create a Public-Facing "Why Cordillera" Content Layer
A dedicated public section of the website — separate from the resident portal — showcasing the community's amenities, neighborhoods, lifestyle, and natural setting. Right now, real estate brokers write the Cordillera story for buyers. The community should own that narrative. This section also gives AI search engines something authoritative to index and cite.
03 Claim and Build a Google Business Profile
Create a verified GBP for the Cordillera community at the CPOA address (360 Carterville Road, Edwards, CO). Complete every field — photos of the Athletic Center, golf courses, trails, and equestrian center. Add amenities, hours, and a link to the community site. A complete GBP is the foundation of Google Maps visibility and directly feeds into AI search citation.
04 Claim TripAdvisor Golf Listings and Launch a Review Strategy
Claim both the Summit Course and Valley Course TripAdvisor listings. Add photos, respond to existing reviews, and post a request in the member newsletter asking golfers to share their experience. The courses have 4.4 and 4.2 ratings — that's strong. 14 and 6 reviews is not. A managed review campaign over one golf season could triple the review count and significantly improve ranking among "Edwards, CO outdoor activities."
05 Transform Instagram Into a Community Showcase
No mountain community has more visually compelling content than Cordillera — morning light on the Nicklaus course, an angler on the Eagle River, aspens in September, a rider at the Equestrian Center, a sunrise from the Summit neighborhood at 9,000 feet. Three posts per week with a consistent aesthetic would build the audience that eventually buys homes here. 847 followers is a baseline, not a ceiling.
06 Address the Lodge & Spa "Permanently Closed" Narrative
The Lodge & Spa at Cordillera's Yelp listing is the most visible signal for any first-time researcher. Even though it's a separate entity, it's the first thing many prospects encounter. The CPOA should add a clear notice on the community website explaining the lodge's status and redirecting prospects to the current amenity offering. A managed community Yelp presence would also help push the closed hotel listing down in relevance.
07 Publish a Seasonal Community Blog — Open to the Public
Quarterly posts — "Summer on the Eagle River," "Golf Season Opens at the Summit Course," "Winter at Cordillera: Gondola Club & Trail Conditions" — give search engines and AI tools something to index, give prospective buyers a window into the lifestyle, and give real estate agents shareable content to use with clients. The existing newsletter can stay resident-only; this would be a separate, public-facing content stream.
