Headline Finding: The Internet Cannot See Your Site

Every public page on getrookd.com, including the homepage, rentals, pricing, FAQ, and how-it-works, ships with an empty body. The HTML that search engines and AI crawlers receive contains a title tag, well-written meta tags, and no content: the homepage delivers exactly one line of visible text, and the rentals page delivers 24 characters. Everything renders in the browser with JavaScript after the page loads. The consequences are already measurable. As of mid-July 2026, Google has indexed one page of the entire site, and the snippet it shows searchers is stale pre-launch splash copy about joining an email list. AI assistants like ChatGPT, Claude, and Perplexity, whose crawlers largely do not execute JavaScript, can see nothing at all. A trust layer that the internet's trust brokers cannot read.

Key Finding

Rookd's constraint is not product, positioning, or engineering skill. It is one architectural decision: rendering every public page in the browser instead of on the server. That single choice is why Google shows one stale page, why AI assistants draw a blank, and why six declared markets show zero footprint. Because the site is already built on Next.js, the framework that does server rendering natively, this is a targeted refactor of the public routes, not a rebuild. Few audits come down to one fix this cleanly.

Method note: pages were fetched exactly as search and AI crawlers receive them (served HTML, no JavaScript execution) in July 2026, alongside Google index checks and public social lookups. Facebook and LinkedIn actively block automated checks, so those findings should be confirmed by loading the profiles directly. Review counts and index status reflect what was publicly visible at audit time.
05 Top Priority Recommendations
01Server-Render the Public Pages
The single highest-leverage fix in this report. Move the marketing pages (home, how-it-works, pricing, FAQ, about) and the public listing pages to server components or static generation in your existing Next.js App Router; the logged-in application can stay exactly as it is. Once listing pages ship real HTML, every rental on the platform becomes an indexable page that can rank for "[property type] for rent in [town]" searches, which is the organic engine every successful listings marketplace runs on.
02Ship robots.txt, a Dynamic Sitemap, and Search Console
Add a real robots.txt and a dynamic sitemap.xml that includes every public listing URL, then verify the site in Google Search Console and submit it. This is an afternoon of work, and it is how Google discovers new listings within hours instead of never. While you are in there, align the canonical tags with the www redirect so every page points to one consistent address.
03Make the Structured Data Match Reality
Remove the 4.8-star aggregate rating until it is backed by reviews a visitor can actually see, because for a company whose entire brand is trust, unverifiable review markup is the one defect you cannot afford, and it carries real penalty risk with Google. Then add FAQPage markup to your FAQ (the content already exists) and fix or remove the broken Facebook link. Your schema ambition is a strength; it just has to be true.
04Build Server-Rendered Market Pages for Your Six Markets
Breckenridge, Vail Valley / Eagle County, Aspen / Pitkin County, Summit County, Denver, and Boulder each deserve a real landing page: current listings, how verification protects renters in that market, local housing context, and an FAQ. Right now those markets exist only inside invisible schema tags. These pages are what let Rookd rank, and get cited by AI assistants, for the exact searches your renters make.
05Launch a Spanish-Language Renter Experience
A large share of the workforce renters in Eagle and Summit counties speaks Spanish at home, and they are the population most exposed to rental scams and least served by Zillow, Craigslist, and Facebook Marketplace. Not one housing platform in your markets serves them in Spanish. A properly translated renter flow and market pages would give Rookd an audience the incumbents structurally ignore, and it fits your verification mission precisely: the people who most need a trust layer.
06Tell the Story: Local Press, LinkedIn, and the Brand Search
Housing is the story in Colorado mountain towns, and a local startup fighting rental scams with verification is exactly what Summit Daily, Vail Daily, and Colorado startup media cover. A founder story plus a LinkedIn company page plus a few earned links would fix your brand search (currently one stale line), start the backlink profile the SEO work needs, and put the "rhymes with looked" pronunciation into other people's mouths instead of only your own.
